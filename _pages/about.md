# Welcome!

I am **Mohamed Khaled**, a Master's student in **Molecular Biology (Bioinformatics Curriculum)** at the **University of Padova, Italy**, currently conducting my research internship at the **Institut Pasteur** in Paris.

My research interests lie at the intersection of **computational biology**, **omics data analysis**, and **machine learning**. I am particularly interested in developing and applying computational methods to understand complex biological systems using high-throughput sequencing data.

Currently, I work on the analysis of **single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq)** data to investigate cell-cell communication in human embryos and stem cell-derived gastruloids. My goal is to uncover molecular mechanisms underlying early human development and contribute to improving in vitro developmental models.

---

My current research focuses on:

## Research Experience

Throughout my academic training, I have had the opportunity to work on several computational biology projects across Europe.

At the **Institut Pasteur (France)**, I analyze single-cell RNA-seq datasets to characterize molecular differences between human embryos and gastruloids generated from induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs).

During my Master's thesis at the **University of Padova (Italy)**, I benchmarked computational tools for retrotransposon expression analysis by generating simulated RNA-seq datasets with known ground truth, enabling quantitative evaluation of family- and locus-level methods.

Previously, I completed a summer internship at **Helmholtz-Zentrum Dresden-Rossendorf (Germany)**, where I contributed to optimizing epidemiological SIR models for COVID-19 transmission by incorporating behavioral data into parameter estimation.

Before transitioning fully into computational biology, I worked as a **Cytogeneticist** and later as a **Vaccine Production Specialist**, gaining extensive laboratory experience in molecular diagnostics, cytogenetics, sterile manufacturing, and GMP environments.
